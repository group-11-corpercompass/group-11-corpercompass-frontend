Ethnic Groups and Languages

Kaduna State is home to a diverse population made up of several ethnic groups, with the Hausa people and Fulani people being among the most prominent. Other groups such as the Gbagyi people and Adara people also contribute to the cultural diversity of the state. The main language spoken is the Hausa language, while the English language is widely used for education, business, and official communication.

Dominant Groups Hausa people and Fulani people (dominant groups)
Minority Groups Gbagyi people, Adara people, and other minority group (mainly in southern kaduna)
Official Language English language - schools, offices, and goverment
Everyday Languages Hausa language (primary), Nigerian Pidgin and local dialects(informal communication)
Kaduna City Context Cosmopolitan - cities like Kaduna host people from many Nigerian states
Cultural Values Respect, Community living, Strong cultural identity
Elders highly respected - greetings are polite and often accompanied by handshakes
